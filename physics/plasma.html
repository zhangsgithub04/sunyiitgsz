Plasma Physics

A rigorous introduction to collective behavior and ionized gases.

1. The Transition to Plasma

As temperature increases, the kinetic energy of atoms eventually overcomes the ionization potential. Unlike a gas, a plasma is a fluid of charged particles that responds to electromagnetic fields.

"A plasma is a quasineutral gas of charged and neutral particles which exhibits collective behavior." â€” Francis F. Chen

2. Debye Shielding

A fundamental property of plasma is its ability to shield out electric fields. If you insert a test charge, the surrounding particles will rearrange themselves to effectively cancel the charge's field within a specific distance.

The Debye Length ($\lambda_D$)

$$\lambda_D = \sqrt{\frac{\epsilon_0 k_B T_e}{n e^2}}$$

Where $T_e$ is electron temperature and $n$ is density. For a substance to be a plasma, the physical dimensions of the system ($L$) must be much larger than $\lambda_D$.

3. Plasma Oscillations

Because particles are charged, a local displacement of electrons creates an electric field that pulls them back. This leads to high-frequency oscillations known as Langmuir Waves.

$$\omega_p = \sqrt{\frac{n e^2}{m_e \epsilon_0}}$$

Plasma Criteria

Criterion Requirement
Size Scale $L \gg \lambda_D$
Plasma Parameter $N_D \gg 1$ (Many particles in a Debye sphere)
Collision Frequency $\omega \tau > 1$ (Electromagnetic forces dominate collisions)
