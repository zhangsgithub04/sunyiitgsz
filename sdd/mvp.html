MVP & Agile Development

"Think big, start small, learn fast." How to launch our Collaborative Whiteboard in weeks, not years.

What is an MVP?

An MVP is the version of a new product that allows a team to collect the maximum amount of validated learning about customers with the least effort. It is about finding the Value Proposition—the one thing users can't live without.

Whiteboard MVP Core Value:

"Two people, in different cities, drawing on the same digital canvas simultaneously without lag."

Everything else (AI shapes, PDF export, templates) is secondary.

Feature Prioritization: The MoSCoW Method

Must Have

Real-time stroke sync, login, single board.

Should Have

Sticky notes, shape library, text tool.

Could Have

AI auto-beautify, custom templates, dark mode.

Won't Have

VR support, integration with 50+ apps (v1).

The Agile Build-Measure-Learn Loop

01

Sprint Backlog

Pulling "Must Have" features into a 2-week development cycle. Each sprint produces a "Potentially Shippable Increment."

02

Daily Standups

Team alignment: What did I do yesterday? What will I do today? Any blockers (e.g., WebSocket latency issues)?

03

Sprint Review

Demonstrating the working MVP to stakeholders and users to get immediate feedback for the next sprint.

Measuring the MVP

Key Metric Why it matters for the Whiteboard Target for V1
Retention Rate Do users come back to use the board again? > 30% weekly
Latency (ms) Does the sync feel "live" enough for collaboration? < 100ms
Viral Coefficient Are users inviting teammates via board links? > 1.2
