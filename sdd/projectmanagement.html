Software Project Management

Navigating the "Triple Constraint" to deliver a high-performance Collaborative Whiteboard on time and within budget.

The PM Foundations

The Iron Triangle

Every project is bound by three competing forces. If you change one, the others must react. For our Whiteboard, if we want to add AI Auto-Drawing (Scope), we either need more Time or more Budget for better GPUs.

  • Scope: The specific features (Drawing, Syncing).
  • Time: The delivery schedule (V1 in 3 months).
  • Cost: The resources (Developers, Cloud Servers).

Quality sits in the center of the triangle.

What makes Software PM unique?

Unlike building a bridge, software is "invisible" and highly volatile. SPM manages Intangibility, Complexity, and Change.

Estimation Complexity

Software tasks are hard to estimate. "Adding a pen tool" might take 2 hours; "Syncing strokes at scale" might take 2 months.

Requirement Volatility

Users might realize they want "Voice Chat" halfway through. SPM uses Agile to embrace this change.

Technical Debt

Managing the long-term "cost" of quick-and-dirty code fixes to meet a whiteboard launch date.

Project Lifecycle Phases

1. Initiation

Feasibility study: Is there a market for a new whiteboard?

2. Planning

Defining WBS (Work Breakdown Structure) and timelines.

3. Execution

The Sprints: Coding, Design, and Integration.

4. Monitoring

Tracking Burn-down charts and server performance.

5. Closing

Handover to Ops and "Post-Mortem" lessons learned.

Visualizing dependencies and milestones across the whiteboard project timeline.

Risk Management

"What if the WebSocket server crashes with 1,000 users?"

Risk Identification

High latency in stroke syncing during peak hours.

Mitigation Strategy

Implement horizontal scaling and load balancing early in development.

Quality Management (QA)

  • Verification: Are we building the product correctly? (Code reviews, Unit tests).
  • Validation: Are we building the right product? (User testing on drawing speed).

PM is the art of getting things done through others.
